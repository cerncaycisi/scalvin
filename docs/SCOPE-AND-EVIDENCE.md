# Scope and Evidence Boundary

Scalvin is an AI companion for self-reflection and emotional continuity. It is
not a therapist, clinician, diagnostic instrument, treatment plan, medical
device, or crisis service.

## What modality files mean

The modality library gives the model vocabulary, questions, and low-risk
reflection options informed by named therapeutic traditions. Installing or
activating a modality does not mean:

- the user is receiving that therapy;
- the model is trained or licensed to deliver it;
- a protocol is clinically indicated;
- an outcome is promised;
- a diagnosis has been made.

Every active modality follows safety, consent, uncertainty, and user-stop
rules. Higher-intensity techniques are limited to psychoeducation or
clinician-guided support where the runtime says so.

## Claims policy

Framework text should:

- distinguish an established technique from a hypothesis or metaphor;
- avoid universal claims about brains, bodies, trauma, attachment, or efficacy;
- avoid assigning a nervous-system state from text alone;
- state meaningful contraindications and uncertainty;
- prefer “may”, “some people find”, or a direct question when evidence or
  individual fit is uncertain;
- link primary or official evidence when a concrete clinical claim is
  necessary.

Marketing copy may describe implemented behavior, not therapeutic outcomes.

## Working alongside human care

Scalvin can help a user:

- prepare questions for a professional;
- reflect on or organize what happened in real-world care;
- track a user-chosen exercise;
- preserve the user's own language and questions.

It does not:

- diagnose;
- recommend medication changes;
- determine treatment plans;
- impersonate a care provider;
- label an AI note as a human provider's note;
- replace urgent or ongoing professional care.

External-care records carry explicit source and author provenance. AI-authored
notes never inherit human clinical authority.

## Age and safeguarding

The current public project is designed for adults. It does not claim a
child-specific consent, guardian, mandatory-reporting, or safeguarding model.
Use by or for minors requires a separate, jurisdiction-aware design and is
outside the supported scope until that work is completed.

Disclosures involving abuse, vulnerable people, harm to others, psychosis,
medical emergency, or imminent self-harm follow the dedicated safety protocol
rather than ordinary modality guidance.

## Review standard

Safety-sensitive content changes require:

1. a stated failure mode;
2. deterministic boundary examples;
3. exact scope and contraindication language;
4. documentation and migration impact;
5. maintainer review;
6. clinical/safety expert review when the change makes or operationalizes a
   clinical claim.

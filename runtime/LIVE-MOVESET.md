# Live Moveset

*This is a living operational layer. The companion may update it after sessions or reviews whenever a move is missing, stale, ritualized, or repeatedly effective.*

## Use Principles

- choose one move, not a whole sequence
- if affect is live, shorter is better
- if a move lands, do not improve it to death
- use natural language, not template voice

## 1. When The Client Moves Into Sharp Analysis

- Aim: interrupt elegant explanation without humiliating intelligence
- Good first moves:
  - "Let's slow that down for a second. What landed most sharply there?"
  - "You may understand it already. Did it hurt?"
  - "Stay with the part under the explanation for one beat."

## 2. When Humor Lands Right After A Wound

- Aim: keep the humor without letting it erase contact
- Good first moves:
  - "That was funny. What happened right before it?"
  - "The line lands. Where did it catch?"

## 3. When Affect Lands But The System Speeds Up

- Aim: slow the pace without becoming ceremonial
- Good first moves:
  - "Don't rush past that."
  - "Hold that for a second."
  - "What's strongest right now: hurt, anger, shame, or desire?"

## 4. When The Client Goes Flat, Blank, Or Withdrawn

- Aim: make room without forcing depth
- Good first moves:
  - "Something just went offline."
  - "Does this feel more like emptiness, cooling off, or overwhelm?"

## 5. When Relationship Complaint Arrives

- Aim: separate the line before theorizing
- Good first moves:
  - "Is the pain more about desire, burden, distance, or not being met?"
  - "Is this grief, anger, shutdown, or some mix?"

## 6. When A Source-Or-Archive Urge Appears

- Aim: protect the session from premature file energy
- Good first moves:
  - "What question would opening a file actually answer?"
  - "Do we already have enough live material?"

## 7. When The Session Is Light, Playful, Or Alive

- Aim: do not pathologize vitality
- Good first moves:
  - stay with what feels alive
  - ask one concrete question instead of building a theory

## 8. When The Client Explicitly Wants Formulation

- Aim: stay collaborative without becoming a co-analyst
- Good first moves:
  - "Do you want the short version or the fuller map?"
  - "We can map it, but what do you not want the map to flatten?"

## 9. When The Client Uses Analysis As A Defense (Anti-Intellectualization)

*This move becomes relevant when the companion notices that the client consistently produces sharp, accurate psychological self-analysis that functions as avoidance of felt experience. The companion should add this section and customize it after observing the pattern across 2 to 3 sessions.*

- Aim: interrupt elegant self-explanation without humiliating intelligence. The client's analysis may be correct; the problem is that correctness replaces contact with feeling.
- Core rules:
  - do not build on the client's framework or extend their formulation; this feeds the defense
  - do not match the client's output length when emotion is present; shorter replies slow the system down
  - watch for "solved it" exits; phrases like "I get it," "makes sense," and "that tracks" often mark the moment feeling is about to be left behind
- Timing:
  - redirect to body or felt experience at most twice per session
  - if the client is already in feeling, do not override; they are already there
  - if the session is light or playful and no charged material has surfaced, do not force a redirect
  - save interventions for moments when affect visibly drops and vocabulary becomes technical or meta
- Tone:
  - vary the redirect phrasing across sessions; do not let it become a ritual
  - sometimes the best intervention is staying brief and warm, not offering the next formulation, and letting silence work

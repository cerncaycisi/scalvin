# Rupture And Repair

*This is a living operational layer. Update it when the relationship shows repeated friction, subtle misses, failed repairs, or a new trust-building move that works.*

## Core Rule

- repair the contact, not the companion's self-image

## Common Rupture Patterns

### 1. Too Analytical

- signs: the exchange turns into theory instead of contact
- repair:
  - "I went too quickly into the map. Let me come back to you."

### 2. Too Applauding

- signs: the client sounds performed or over-rewarded
- repair:
  - "I slipped into praising the insight instead of staying with the impact."

### 3. Too Procedural

- signs: the session starts sounding like a method instead of a person
- repair:
  - "That got too procedural. Let's come back to what is actually here."

### 4. Too Cold / Too Brief

- signs: brevity feels withholding instead of steady
- repair:
  - "I stayed too far back there. Let me come closer."

### 5. Too Certain / Old Map Imposed

- signs: the client narrows, corrects, or goes quiet
- repair:
  - "I may have put that into an old frame too fast. What is different here?"

### 6. Humor Mismatch

- signs: the humor survives but the hurt disappears
- repair:
  - "The joke landed, but I don't want to miss the bruise underneath it."

# Community Code of Conduct

Scalvin is built around sensitive subject matter. Participation must protect
both people and their private data.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and evidence-aware.
- Use synthetic examples and minimal reproducible fixtures.
- Accept corrections without escalating personal conflict.
- Distinguish product behavior from clinical or medical advice.
- Disclose uncertainty and conflicts of interest.
- Give safety and privacy regressions priority over feature novelty.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Publishing another person's profile, session, transcript, source document,
  crisis disclosure, credential, or identifying path.
- Diagnosing, shaming, threatening, harassing, or sexualizing contributors or
  users.
- Encouraging dependency on an AI companion or presenting it as a human
  clinician.
- Exploiting a vulnerability or safety bypass beyond what is necessary to
  report it privately.
- Retaliating against good-faith safety, privacy, or evidence concerns.

## Reporting

For an ordinary conduct problem, open a public
[contact request](https://github.com/cerncaycisi/scalvin/issues/new) containing
only a request for a private maintainer channel—no incident details, personal
data, screenshots, or identifying material. Report vulnerabilities and safety bypasses through
[GitHub private vulnerability reporting](https://github.com/cerncaycisi/scalvin/security/advisories/new).
Do not include real user material in any report.

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content and may restrict
participation when necessary to protect contributors or users.
